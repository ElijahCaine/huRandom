About

In October of 2014 it dawned upon me that I had no idea what it took to justify a number being 'random' or 'non-random'. Wikipedia defines Statistical randomness as being 'A numeric sequence containing no recognizable patterns or regularities.'; for example, the digits of Ï€ and the result of a dice roll are statistically random.

Around this time I was also learning about the Linux special files `/dev/random` and `/dev/urandom`; one of which streams out truly random data while the other streams out psuedo-random data. Although I knew from looking at their documentation that one was truly random and the other was not when you print out the values produced by both there was no difference.

This got me to ask the question "How random is the human random number generator?" I would assume we're not truly random because we love to find and use patterns even in things that don't have any, but if you got a crowd of tens, hundreds, or thousands of people to give you their own 'random' number would it be a numeric sequence containing no recognizable patterns or regularities, or would it hold patterns and regularities? This is why I created the HuRandom project.

Open Everything

HuRandom is 100% Open and Free. All of the code is available on Github under The MIT License. Transparency is also key, not only in how the data is collected for HuRandom, but also the data itself, so our entire dataset is available under a Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 International license. Of course you are trusting that the publicly available code is the code I'm using, and that the data I make available is actually the entirety of the data I collect, and that I'm not injecting a virus into your computer right now... but these are risks we each take every day; I can only claim transparency, it's up to you to choose weather or not to trust me and the HuRandom project.

Technical

HuRandom is a webapp built with the Flask Python Microframework and uses Pygal to generate graphs, Bootstrap CSS for the interface. It is deployed with Docker on CentOS 6 Digital Ocean servers, more info on deploying and development can be found in the README.
